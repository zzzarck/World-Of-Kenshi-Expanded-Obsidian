These are the empires that have sent waves of colonizers to the planet, they are massive and pay little attention to what is happening in this world, most of them having a very poor micro-administrative system, focusing on local planet rulers.

Concord of Sol
--
The original rulers of the galaxy, they own earth and the solar system as their capital, while they were once the rulers of the galaxy now they face extreme overpopulation and a very slow and inefficient administrative system based around a strict and outdated voting system. there's no sole ruler in the Concord of Sol, bur rather many sector elected governors that vote on what is the best path to take as an empire, due to the now gigantic "hierarchy" system on votes that go down to local, planet province owners, it takes years to make simple decisions. Not only that, but due to the poor administration, what was once the ruler of the galaxy was forced to grant independence to many smaller empires, this lead to wars and the eventual state the galaxy is now on.

The Concord of Sol arrived to the planet due to the need of housing for the increasing overpopulation that it faces, having sent colonizers to every planet that was remotely inhabitable, many were lost under thousands of logs about every single planet, so they decided to give almost full autonomy to the colonizers as long as they managed to take control of the planet eventually, when they lost contact with the planet they didn't even realize, and when contact was stablished again they just thought it was a planet that flew under the radar and sent new colonizers without a second thought, and left them to the same, autonomous fate.

The Accord of Free Sectors
--
The first sector that directly opposed the Concord after the first partition, it aims not to destroy democracy, but to update the old and sluggish Concord administrative system, ever since the partition, they have reduced the time it takes to make decisions, removing people from the voting hierarchy, and often giving permission for higher ups to take direct action on many decisions. They also were one of the first sectors to act as a spark to the Galaxy Wide Wars, attacking its neighbours that did not willingly follow their views, and integrating them into the Accord, however, the system that they upgraded only worked for a few hundred years, now that the Accord takes a much bigger place on the galaxy, colonies face instability due to anger from decisions taken without the people's vote, and many decisions taken by higher ups that ended up to be outright wrong, this had led to chaos and local wars across the Accord.

The Accord arrived at the planet right after the Concord as they both had a common border with it, aiming to take control over the planet faster than Concord, even if it meant they had to use force to do so, when they eventually lost communication with the colony, while worried at the start, it eventually went into the logs of destroyed and lost colonies as the Accord had many other things to worry about, when the planet appeared on the map again, the old logs and information about it were long lost in a local war, making them send colonizers without any worry. The new colonizers are overviewed by a team that goes in for reports every few years, to make sure the colony does not get out of hand.

People's Welfare Union
--
The original sector of this federation was let go in the first partition, its people had always been peaceful and enjoyed extremely good living standard and welfare, but once they were cut off from economic support of the concord, they were desperate for survival, however, nearby sectors were facing the same desperation and united under one federation. keeping the same administrative system of Concord, but on a much smaller scale allowed them to stablish a much more efficient vote hierarchy, where every voice was heard, focusing on building inwards rather than outwards, however as centuries passed, its people became increasingly xenophobic towards other ideologies and outsiders in general since they got so used to their isolation in the first few centuries after the federation's formation, Now they face increasing claims of nearby sectors with not too much of a force to defend itself, all due to the growing hate of the people inside the federation towards outsiders.

The PWU were the only ones to not send colonizers in the first wave, only after the planet dissapeared from the maps, and appeared again, they were centralized enough to even think about colonizing, and even now, they had no idea other nations were in the process of colonizing this planet already, having come right after the accord and Concord, the colonizers will now have to deal with the presence of outsiders on a planet they were supposed to conquer peacefully.

The Meritate Union
--
The original sector of the meritate comes from the 2nd partition of The Concord, after the first one they already knew there was another one coming, they prepared drafts of action before it even happened, and once it did, they were quick to act, they starved nearby sectors to force them into their sphere of influence, after that they used their newfound power to completely change the administration system, the sector governors were now elected from the merit of their research and inventions, and were also allowed to take decisions independently, the people that didn't like this were not able to fight back not due to oppression, but due to dependency on the meritate. As the centuries passed the Meritate focused more on the integration of AI in every task, making merit more and more important as the years went by, now, merit is the only thing that defines a person, if you are not important enough you die in the streets of a city.

The Meritate approached the planet with a set plan to exploit as many resources as it could, once landed, the AI commanded expedition advanced rapidly, with both automatons and humans doing the field work, when communications were cut off, the same phenomena also affected the AI that was commanding the colonization, it was not able to report any errors to The Meritate, and when the planet appeared back, it continued as if nothing happened, just sending a new scheduled party to colonize a new planet after redrafting the plan.

The Vigil
--
The vigil is an organization that originates from the 2nd partition of The Accord, once the sector they resided in got cutoff, they feared the worst and started expanding their reach independently from the sector governor, once they grew they started getting surveillance outposts running in other sectors, controlling trade routes and at times even heavily influencing politics in many factions. as they grew they started influencing more and more factions, these started to fear The vigil and decided to let them be due to the economical and political danger it meant to destroy them. The vigil now owns entire fleets dedicated to private security and giant habitats made for surveillance and other, not very legal things. It is run by a group of people that pass information and missions as they die, focusing on professionalism and following of orders even after the leaders die.

The vigil is technically the first to interact with the planet, sending the first group of people to it, not to colonize but to stablish hidden networks in case anyone tried to colonize it. When contact with the planet dissapeared, they were the only faction that realized the planet dissapeared completely, they stationed a highly advanced ship next to it with the sole purpose of investigating what had happened, and when the planet suddenly reappeared, they were quick to send teams to gather as much information as possible, now they have stablished many small outposts around the planet that count on the ship stationed near it to provide them support.
